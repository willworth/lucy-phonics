# Mentava Alphabet Sounds — Analysis

**Source:** "Mentava's Alphabet Sounds" PDF by Niels (founder of Mentava)
**What it is:** A phonemic awareness picture book for preschoolers, designed as a precursor to their learn-to-read software.

---

## Core Philosophy

Mentava's approach aligns with synthetic phonics but has its own emphasis:

1. **Phonemic awareness first, reading second.** The book doesn't teach reading directly — it teaches *hearing and distinguishing sounds*. Recognition before recall. This is the foundation everything else builds on.

2. **Sounds, not letter names.** Explicitly says: if your child hasn't learned letter names ("ay, bee, see") — *don't teach them*. Call letters by their sounds ("aaa, mmm, sss"). Letter names cause confusion when blending.

3. **Consonants without the schwa.** Critical pronunciation note: say "b-" not "buh", "t-" not "tuh". We don't say "buh-aaa-tuh" for "bat." Practice clean sound slicing.

4. **Fun over drilling.** This is quality time with a parent, not homework. Short, playful, no pressure.

### Phase 2: Consonant Digraphs

| Sound | Example | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| sh | "shell" | The "shhh" sound |
| ch | "cheese" | The "ch" sound |
| th (voiced) | "this" | Fingers on throat → vibrates |
| th (unvoiced) | "thin" | Fingers on throat → stays still |
| ng | "ring" | Hard to say alone, always with a vowel: "ing", "ang", "ong" |
| zh | "treasure" | Like "sh" but voiced (throat vibrates) |

### Phase 3: Long Vowels & Vowel Teams

| Sound | Example | Spellings |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| ae | "cake" | a_e, ai, ay, eigh, ea |
| ee | "bee" | ee, ea, ey, ie, y |
| ie | "eye" | i_e, ie, igh, y |
| oe | "go" | o_e, oa, ow, o |
| ue/oo | "moon" | oo, ue, ui, ew, ou |

### Phase 4: Other Vowels

| Sound | Example | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| oo (short) | "book" | Different from "moon" |
| ue (yoo) | "unicorn" | The "you" sound |

### Phase 5: Diphthongs

| Sound | Example |
|-------|---------|
| ow | "cow" |
| oi | "boy" |

### Phase 6: R-Controlled Vowels

| Sound | Example | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------|
| ar | "car" | "cat" vs "car" — the r changes everything |
| or | "corn" | "con" vs "corn" |
| er/ir/ur | "bird" | All three spelled differently, pronounced the same |

### Special Case: Raised 'a'

The 'a' before 'n' or 'm' becomes more nasal in American English. "man" vs "cat" are different. Important because "am" will be one of the first words read. Not explicitly taught — just noted.

## Sound Comparison Pairs

This is the gold for the app. Mentava dedicates a large section to confusable sounds with explicit side-by-side comparisons:

### Consonant Pairs
| Pair | Example words |
|------|---------------|
| l vs r | lamp/ramp, light/right, lip/rip |
| w vs v | wine/vine, west/vest, wet/vet |
| s vs th | sink/think, sank/thank, sick/thick |
| d vs th | day/they, doe/though, den/then |
| b vs v | bat/vat, ban/van, bow/vow |
| s vs sh | sip/ship, sore/shore, sue/shoe |
| ch vs sh | chip/ship, chop/shop, chew/shoe |
| j vs y | jess/yes, jam/yam, gel/yell |
| f vs p | fat/pat, fin/pin, fool/pool |
| g vs k | gold/cold, gate/kate, goat/coat |

### Vowel Pairs
| Pair | Example words |
|------|---------------|
| e vs i | set/sit, bet/bit, pen/pin |
| a vs e | bat/bet, pat/pet, pan/pen |
| o vs u | hot/hut, cop/cup, lock/luck |
| aw vs o | saw/sew, law/low, caught/coat |
| oo vs u | pool/pull, fool/full, food/foot |

### The Big Grid (Minimal Pairs)

Mentava includes a brilliant grid showing how changing ONE vowel changes the word entirely:

**bat → bet → boat → bot → beat → bite → bit → bait**

And similar grids for: pin/pen/pine/pan, moose/moss/mouse/miss/mess/mice, etc.

This is exactly what the app needs — systematic minimal pair exercises.

## Developmental Readiness

Mentava includes a readiness test using emoji pairs:

- Show the child 🐶🐟 and say "dogfish"
- Show 🐟🐶 and say "fishdog"
- Can they tell which is which?

**If not, they're not developmentally ready for left-to-right reading.** Don't push it — just keep doing sound recognition.

### Pronunciation Milestones (approximate ages)

| Age | Sounds they can typically pronounce |
|-----|-------------------------------------|
| 2 | Vowels, P, B, M, D, N, H, T |
| 3 | K, G, W, NG, F, Y |
| 4 | L, J, CH, S, V, SH, Z |
| Last | R, voiced TH, unvoiced TH, ZH |

**Key insight:** A child can *hear and distinguish* sounds before they can *pronounce* them correctly. Bad pronunciation is fine — don't correct it aggressively.

## Differences from Jolly Phonics (what we had in README)

| Aspect | Jolly Phonics | Mentava |
|--------|---------------|---------|
| **Order** | s,a,t,p,i,n first (build words fast) | Roughly alphabetical |
| **Goal** | Reading from day one (blending immediately) | Phonemic awareness first, reading later |
| **Blending** | Introduced with first group | Deferred until developmentally ready |
| **When to start reading** | Implicit: ASAP | Explicit: test readiness with emoji pairs |
| **Sound comparisons** | Not a major focus | Entire section dedicated to confusable pairs |

### Recommendation for the App

**Use Mentava's methodology** — it's more appropriate for Lucy (age 3) because:
1. She may not be developmentally ready for blending yet — Mentava's awareness-first approach is safer
2. The sound comparison pairs are incredibly valuable and not in Jolly Phonics
3. The approach is more relaxed — quality time, not drilling
4. It explicitly addresses the "don't teach letter names" principle

**But steal Jolly Phonics' early word-building** — once she passes the readiness test (emoji pairs), switch to introducing sounds in an order that enables blending (s,a,t,p,i,n). Best of both worlds.

## Implications for App Design

### Phase 1: Sound Recognition (Mentava's approach)
- Introduce sounds one at a time with images
- "This says /s/! Can you find the ssssnake?"
- Audio of clean, schwa-free sounds is CRITICAL
- No reading, no letters-to-words yet

### Phase 2: Sound Discrimination (Mentava's comparison pairs)
- "Which one says /b/? Which one says /d/?"
- Minimal pair games: hear "bat" — is it bat or dat?
- Start with easy pairs (s vs sh), progress to hard (e vs i)

### Phase 3: Readiness Test
- Built-in emoji pair test: 🐶🐟 vs 🐟🐶
- If she passes → unlock blending activities
- If not → stay in Phases 1-2, retest monthly

### Phase 4: Blending (post-readiness)
- NOW use Jolly Phonics order: s, a, t, p, i, n
- Sound out words: s...a...t → "sat"
- Picture matching with blended words

### Audio Requirements
- **Every sound needs a clean recording** — no schwa
- Will recording them himself would be ideal (familiar voice, English accent)
- Fallback: find professional phoneme audio clips
- Each sound also needs example words with the sound emphasized
